For production companies, machine breakdowns are a difficult issue. Machine breakdowns can lead to costly production stops and should ideally be avoided. One approach to this is the early prediction of failures so that maintenance measures can be initiated in good time.

In the following experiment, an artificial intelligence (AI) algorithm is trained to predict machine breakdowns. The AI algorithm has a binary output that indicates whether a particular machine is going to break down within the next 48 hours or not.

When the AI algorithm makes a correct prediction, we therefore differentiate between two outcomes:

  • Correct prediction of a breakdown.
  • Correct prediction of smooth operations (no breakdown).

Although being properly trained on this task, the AI algorithm is not entirely perfect and makes false predictions occasionally. Such false predictions can appear in two different flavors:

  • False alarm: the AI algorithm predicts a machine breakdown but the machine is operating smoothly in the next 48 hours.
  • Missed hit: the AI algorithm predicts smooth operations but the machine breaks down in the next 48 hours.
