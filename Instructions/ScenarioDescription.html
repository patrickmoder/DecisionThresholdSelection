Machine breakdowns at production companies can lead to costly production stops, while too early maintenance leads to unnecessary maintenance cost. One approach to this is the early prediction of machine failures, so that maintenance measures can be initiated at the right time. This approach trades off the two costs.

In the following experiment, an artificial intelligence (AI) algorithm is trained to predict machine breakdowns. The AI algorithm has a binary (yes/no) output that indicates whether a particular machine is going to break down within the next 48 hours or not.

Correct predictions of the AI algorithm can be of two types:

  • Correct prediction of a machine's breakdown.
  • Correct prediction of a machine's smooth operations (no breakdown).

Although being properly trained on this task, the AI algorithm is not entirely perfect and makes false predictions occasionally. Such false predictions can also be of two types:

  • False alarm: the AI algorithm predicts a machine's breakdown but the machine is operating smoothly in the next 48 hours.
  • Missed hit: the AI algorithm predicts a machine's smooth operations but the machine breaks down in the next 48 hours.
