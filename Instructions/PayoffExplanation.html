In the following experiment, your task will be to choose a decision threshold in multiple rounds for different classification scenarios. Your bonus payoff will depend on how close your chosen threshold is to the optimal threshold Dopt, that is the threshold that minimizes overall costs.

Let us illustrate the bonus payoff calculation again with our previous example. The AI algorithm predicted which class (positive or negative) an object belongs to. Out of a set of 20 objects, 10 objects belonged to the positive class and 10 objects belonged to the negative class. Costs for misclassifications were 5$ for each False Positive (FP) classification and 1$ for each False Negative (FN) classification, respectively. With our selected threshold D, the algorithm made 13 correct classifications (accuracy 0.65). Moreover, misclassification costs were 3 x 5$ (=15$) for False Positive classifications and 4 x 1$ (=4$) for False Negative clsasifications, resulting in 19$ overall.

Now, let us assume that the optimal threshold Dopt is different from our previously chosen threshold D. In fact, with Dopt we expect the following outcomes, also displayed in the confusion matrix below:

  • 12 correct classifications: 4 True Positives, 8 True Negatives
  • 8 incorrect classifications: 2 False Positives and 6 False Negatives

For the optimal threshold Dopt we would expect misclassification costs of 2 x 5$ (=10$) for False Positive classifications and 6 x 1$ (=6$) for False Negative classifications, resulting in 16$ overall. As we can see, we expect less overall costs than with the previously chosen threshold (19$). Comparing all possible thresholds and their resulting misclassification costs, we find that 16$ is the lowest number possible (the optimum) and therefore the corresponding threshold is named Dopt. We display the overall misclassification costs for Dopt in the cost matrix below.

Note that the overall number of correct classifications with the optimal threshold Dopt is 12 (accuracy 0.60) and therefore below the number of correct classifications (13, accuracy 0.65) with our previously selected threshold D. Despite Dopt being suboptimal regarding accuracy, remember that the optimal threshold Dopt is always the threshold that minimizes costs in our setting.
