For the AI algorithm, the balance between positive and negative classifications can be adjusted. This adjustment results in a trade-off between the four outcomes in the confusion matrix. An increase in one column sum consequently leads to a decrease in the other column sum, while the total numbers of TP+FN and TN+FP (the row sums) stay the same.

This adjustment can be made by varying the decision threshold D. One can set D between [0;1]:

  • The larger the threshold D, the more objects are classified as negative, i.e., more TN and FN.
  • The smaller the threshold D, the more objects are classified as positive, i.e., more TP and FP.
