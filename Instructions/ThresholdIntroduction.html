Before applying the AI algorithm in a real-world setting, the balance between breakdown- and no-breakdown-predictions can be adjusted.

The AI algorithm outputs a certain breakdown-likelihood for each particular machine in the next 48 hours. One can vary the decision threshold D between 0 and 1 to indicate the breakdown-likelihood above which machines shall be predicted as being at risk of breakdown. As a result, the likelihood output by the AI algorithm is translated into a binary prediction: if the likelihood is above D, we predict a breakdown in the next 48 hours; while if below D, we predict no breakdown in the next 48 hours. The adjustment of D results in the following trade-off:

  • The smaller the threshold D, the more often breakdowns will be predicted.
  • The larger the threshold D, the less often breakdowns will be predicted.
