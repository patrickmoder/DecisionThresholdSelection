In the following experiment, your task will be to adjust an AI algorithm that is applied to predict breakdowns of your 10 machines. In every of the 13 rounds, you will receive information on:

  • The sample size of the training set that was used to train the AI algorithm.
  • How many machines in the training set break down in the next 48 hours and how many do not.
  • Misclassification costs after False Alarms and Missed Hits.
  • The AI algorithm's expected prediction outcomes for the training set.

Your task is to choose a decision threshold for the AI algorithm. After you confirm your choice, the AI algorithm gets applied to predict breakdowns of your 10 machines in the next 48 hours.

Let us illustrate the task with our previous example. Misclassification costs were 5 cost units for each False Alarm and 1 cost unit for each Missed Hit, respectively. Remember that when choosing a decision threshold D of 0.65, predictions by the AI algorithm for the 20 machines on the training set are as follows:

  • 13 correct predictions: 6 Correct Predictions of Breakdown, 7 Correct Predictions of No Breakdown
  • 7 incorrect predictions: 3 False Alarms and 4 Missed Hits

Let us assume that Jane chooses D = 0.65 for this round. When the AI algorithm gets applied to predict breakdowns of Jane's 10 machines in the next 48 hours, the prediction outcomes for this particular round are as follows (also displayed in the matrix below):
  • 8 correct predictions: 3 Correct Predictions of Breakdown, 5 Correct Predictions of No Breakdown
  • 2 incorrect predictions: 1 False Alarm and 1 Missed Hit

Prediction Outcomes after applying the AI algorithm
Predicted State
Breakdown No Breakdown
Actual State Breakdown 3 1
No Breakdown 1 5

Consequently, after applying the AI algorithm, misclassification costs of 1 x 5 cost units (=5 cost units) for False Alarms and 1 x 1 cost units (=1 cost unit) for Missed Hits get realized, resulting in 6 cost units overall. Jane's (and your) bonus payoff for every round will depend on the misclassification costs after applying the AI algorithm. We will explain its calculation on the next page.
