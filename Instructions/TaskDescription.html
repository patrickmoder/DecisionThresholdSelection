In the following experiment, your task will be to adjust the threshold D of an AI algorithm that is applied to predict breakdowns of machines. You will play 18 rounds. In every of the 18 rounds, you will receive information on:

  • The sample size of the training set that was used to train the AI algorithm.
  • How many machines in the training set broke down in the subsequent 48 hours and how many did not.
  • Costs for incorrect predictions that can be different for False Alarms and Missed Hits.
  • The AI algorithm's expected prediction outcomes for the training set.

Your task is to choose a threshold D for the AI algorithm. Then, this configuration is used to predict breakdowns of 10 machines you are responsible of. For these 10 machines, you can expect that the data in the training set is representative of future breakdowns. You can use a slider to enter your value of D. After you confirm your choice, the AI algorithm will predict breakdowns of the 10 machines in the next 48 hours based on your threshold D.

Let us illustrate the task with our previous example. Costs for incorrect predictions are 5 cost units for each False Alarm and 1 cost unit for each Missed Hit, respectively. Remember from the previous screen that when choosing a threshold D of 0.65, predictions by the AI algorithm for the 20 machines in the training set were as follows:

  • 13 correct predictions: 6 Correct Predictions of Breakdown, 7 Correct Predictions of No Breakdown
  • 7 incorrect predictions: 3 False Alarms and 4 Missed Hits
Let us assume that you chose D = 0.65 for this round. When the AI algorithm is applied to predict breakdowns of the 10 machines in the next 48 hours, the prediction outcomes for this particular round would be as follows (also displayed in the matrix below):
  • 8 correct predictions: 3 Correct Predictions of Breakdown, 5 Correct Predictions of No Breakdown
  • 2 incorrect predictions: 1 False Alarm and 1 Missed Hit

Prediction Outcomes after applying the AI algorithm
Predicted State
Breakdown No Breakdown
Actual State Breakdown 3 1
No Breakdown 1 5

Consequently, after applying the AI algorithm, the following costs for incorrect predictions are realized:
   1 x 5 cost units (=5 cost units) for False Alarms
+ 1 x 1 cost units (=1 cost unit) for Missed Hits
= 6 cost units.

Your bonus payoff for every round will depend on the costs for incorrect predictions after applying the AI algorithm. We will explain its calculation on the next page.
